Saving the Earth From Ozone Pollution

Sukur / 10-15-2020 / Version 0.1

Saving the ozone layer, we have saved the environment. Although there may consist of some jokes, it can still reveal the important state of ozone layer comparing to other part of Earth so that we can conclude that ozone layer crisis stays in the front of environmental crisis. “Thirty-two years ago, countries signed the world’s most successful environmental treaty, the Montreal Protocol. That’s the treaty that saved the ozone layer, saved millions of lives, and avoided a global catastrophe.”





As what was told in the new documentary Ozone Hole, the story begins nearly 50 years ago when two chemists, Sherwood Rowland and Mario Molina, discovered that chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) released from aerosol sprays could rise miles over our heads into the stratosphere. In that place, the chemical reaction will happen causing a harmful chemical gas went out, which would have condemned millions of people worldwide to die from skin cancer, go blind with cataracts, or suffer from immune diseases.



For more than a decade, the chemical companies that made CFCs reacted much like today’s coal and oil companies: They denied the science, attacked the scientists, and predicted economic ruin.



But scientists and lawyers at NRDC—well before I got here—fought back. They helped Rowland and Molina tell their story to Congress and the news media. They pushed for bans on CFC aerosols here at home and pressed the United States to demand the same from other countries.



Sweden was the first country to cross the line of written endorsement and accelerate its timetable for the abolition of CFCS.In June 1988, the Riksdag passed legislation to incinerate CFCS in 1995.After extensive discussions with industry, the Government of Sweden has established a schedule for phasing out frozen sterilizers and continuing use of CFCS sprays until 1988, with a packaging life of 1989;The service life of solvent and foam foam is 1991, and the maximum crown life for hard foam foam, dry cleaning and coolant is 1994.Sweden USES only 1% of the world's chlorofluorocarbons (CFCS), so there is an urgent need for other countries, especially big ambassadors, to look to Sweden as a prime example to follow.
