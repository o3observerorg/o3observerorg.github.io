The Destruction of Ozone Layer: Ecosystem Effects

Susan / 10-15-2020 / Version 0.1

Without the protection of ozone layer, large amount of ultraviolet light can penetrate through the atmosphere, and it can destroy the structure of DNA, RNA, and proteins inside organisms, leading them undergoing mutations. All of these will lead to decrease in biodiversity which can cause many unpredictable negative impacts. The enhancement of ultraviolet radiation will disrupt the complex food chain in the ecosystem, leading to the extinction of some major biological species. A large amount of ultraviolet radiation may also reduce the reproductive capacity of marine organisms, disturb the mating habits of insects, and destroy plants, especially crops, which can reduce the yield of crops on the earth by 2/3. Scientific experiments have confirmed that if you add 25% UV radiation to soybeans, the yield will be reduced by 25%. The preliminary results of the study on soybean showed that UV radiation made it more vulnerable to weeds and pests. The reduction of ozone layer thickness by 25% can reduce soybean yield by 20% - 25%, and the protein content and oil content of soybean will also be reduced. If cattle are fed large amounts of UV irradiated grass, the risk of skin and eye cancer will increase.





The indirect effects of harmful ultraviolet radiation like the change of plant morphology, biomass distribution in different parts of plants, the time of development and secondary metabolism, may be as great or even more serious as that caused by harmful ultraviolet radiation. These have potential impacts on the competitive balance of plants, herbivores, plant pathogens and biogeochemical cycles.

In addition, the enhancement of ultraviolet radiation will also affect the growth rate of algae in the ocean, thus indirectly affecting the entire aquatic ecosystem, posing a potential threat to the aquatic ecosystem. For example, the excessive radiation of harmful ultraviolet rays will cause harm to marine organisms within 20 meters depth, which will cause a large number of death of plankton, juvenile fish, crabs, shrimps and shellfish, resulting in the reduction or extinction of some organisms. As any organism in the ocean is an important part of the marine food chain, the reduction or extinction of some species will cause damage to the marine ecosystem. In the global warming aspect, the amount of carbon dioxide absorbed by phytoplankton will also be increased, leading to increase in global temperature.



Ground-level Ozone affects sensitive vegetation and ecosystems, including forests, parks, wildlife refuges and wilderness areas. In particular, ozone harms sensitive vegetation during the growing season. When the ozone gets into the plant leaves, it will reduce photosynthesis, which is a very vital process can produce energy for plants to live and grow. Also, the ability of plants producing and storing food will also be affected, leading to the consequence that plants will have a higher possibility of being occupied by insects and undergoing death. This will consequently decrease the biodiversity and lose of animal habitats.
